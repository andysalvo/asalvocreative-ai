01

LLM (Large Language Model)

Every major AI tool -- ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini -- is powered by an LLM. It predicts what word comes next based on everything it has ever read.

02

Token

LLMs do not process words. They process tokens -- small chunks of text, roughly three-quarters of a word on average.

03

Context Window

The total amount of information an AI model can hold and work with at one time. When it fills up, the model starts forgetting.

04

Inference

Training is when an AI model learns. Inference is when it answers. Every response costs real computation on real hardware.

05

Hallucination

When an LLM lacks a good answer, it does not say "I am not sure." It generates something that sounds right, even if it is completely false.

06

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

An LLM does not know what happened yesterday or have access to private data. RAG lets it look things up before answering.

07

Context Engineering

A prompt is the instruction. Context engineering is everything around it -- the information, examples, and constraints the model sees before responding.

08

Agentic Workflow

A chatbot answers questions. An agent accomplishes goals -- deciding what tools to use, what order to work in, and when the job is done.

09

Guardrails

The technical boundaries set to keep AI operating within acceptable limits. Three layers: input filters, output validation, scope limitation.

10

Human-in-the-Loop

A system design where a human reviews or approves AI decisions at critical points. The central question: where do you place the checkpoints?
