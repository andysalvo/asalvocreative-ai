Inference

Training is when an AI model learns. Inference is when it answers. Every time you send a message to ChatGPT or Claude, the system runs billions of calculations on GPUs in a data center to generate your response. That process is called inference.

This is why AI costs money on an ongoing basis. Training happens once. But inference happens millions of times a day, and the bill scales directly with usage.

Anthropic spent $4.1 billion on training compute in 2025 and $2.7 billion on inference. OpenAI spent $8.7 billion on inference through Azure in just the first three quarters of 2025. Across the industry, compute accounts for 54 to 62 percent of total costs.

Three forces push inference costs up simultaneously. More users every month. Larger models requiring more computation per query. And new patterns like agents multiplying calls per task.

Every major AI lab is currently unprofitable, spending two to three times more than they bring in. The prices users pay today are artificially low. The $20 per month subscription is not covering the actual cost of generating responses.
