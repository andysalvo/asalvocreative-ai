Human-in-the-Loop

Human-in-the-loop means a human reviews, approves, or overrides what the AI does at specific points in a process. The question every organization has to answer is where to place these checkpoints.

Right now, most organizations are getting this wrong. Only 27 percent say employees review all AI-generated content before it reaches customers. A similar share admits that 20 percent or less of output gets checked at all.

51 percent of organizations using AI have experienced at least one negative consequence. Nearly a third trace the problem to inaccuracy -- the AI got something wrong and nobody caught it.

McKinsey found that high-performing organizations shift oversight earlier in the process. They are nearly twice as likely to involve legal early and build risk reviews into development, not deployment.

Governance structures are still thin. Only 28 percent of organizations say their CEO oversees AI governance. Just 17 percent give that to the board. Most organizations are deploying AI faster than they are building the oversight structures to manage it.
