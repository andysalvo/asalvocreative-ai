LLM (Large Language Model)

Every major AI tool -- ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini -- is powered by an LLM. It predicts what word comes next based on everything it has ever read. The reason it feels intelligent is that predicting language well requires understanding context, logic, and reasoning.

The "large" part refers to parameters -- internal variables the model adjusts during training. GPT-3 had 175 billion. GPT-4 reportedly uses 1.76 trillion through a Mixture of Experts architecture: eight sub-models, each 220 billion parameters, with a routing layer that sends each query to the right specialists. But bigger is not always smarter -- Google's Chinchilla outperformed GPT-3 at less than half the size by training on better data.
