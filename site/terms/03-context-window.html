Context Window

The context window is the total amount of information an AI model can hold and work with at one time. Everything you paste in, everything you type, everything it says back -- all of that lives inside the context window. When it fills up, the model starts forgetting earlier parts of the conversation.

Think of it as a desk. A bigger desk means more documents open at once. 128,000 tokens is roughly 50,000 words -- about the length of The Great Gatsby. Move up to 1,000,000 tokens and you can fit a full codebase or a semester of lecture transcripts. The newest models go to 10 million tokens.

As of early 2026, Claude, GPT-4.1, and Gemini 2.5 Pro all offer up to 1 million tokens. But advertised capacity and effective capacity are not the same thing. Most models perform best at 60 to 70 percent of their stated maximum. The reason is the "lost in the middle" problem -- models pay the most attention to what is at the beginning and end. The middle gets blurry.

Cost varies enormously. DeepSeek V3 charges $0.27 per million tokens. Claude Opus 4.5 charges $25. That is nearly a 100x difference. Routing simple queries to cheaper models can cut costs 40 to 60 percent.
